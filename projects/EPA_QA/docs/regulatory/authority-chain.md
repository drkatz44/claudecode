# Regulatory Authority Chain

## Hierarchy (top-down)
Requirements flow downward; traceability flows upward.

### 1. Code of Federal Regulations (CFR)
Primary regulatory authority for EPA QA requirements.

Key CFR titles/parts (to be populated with specific citations):
- **40 CFR** - Protection of Environment (EPA's primary title)
  - Part 136 - Guidelines Establishing Test Procedures for the Analysis of Pollutants (CWA methods)
  - Part 141 - National Primary Drinking Water Regulations
  - Part 258 - Criteria for Municipal Solid Waste Landfills
  - Part 300 - National Oil and Hazardous Substances Pollution Contingency Plan (CERCLA/Superfund)
  - Part 40 - Protection of Environment (general)
- **2 CFR Part 200** - Uniform Administrative Requirements (grants/cooperative agreements)
- Other relevant CFR parts TBD based on project scope

### 2. Federal Register Notices
EPA rulemaking, method promulgation, and policy notices.
- Method updates and new method promulgation
- Regulatory determinations (e.g., PFAS regulatory actions)
- Monitoring requirements

### 3. EPA Orders & Directives
- **EPA Order CIO 2105.0** (or current) - Policy and Program Requirements for the Mandatory Agency-Wide Quality System
- **EPA QA/R-5** - EPA Requirements for Quality Assurance Project Plans
- **EPA QA/G-5** - Guidance on Quality Assurance Project Plans
- **EPA QA/R-2** - EPA Requirements for Quality Management Plans
- Other QA/R and QA/G series documents

### 4. Office-Level Policies
- Office of Applied Science and Environmental Solutions policies
- Division/branch-level procedures
- (Documents to be provided)

### 5. Quality Assurance Project Plans (QAPPs)
- Project-specific implementation of all above requirements
- Defines DQOs, methods, QC criteria, reporting
- References applicable SOPs

### 6. Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)
- Method-specific step-by-step procedures
- QC acceptance criteria at the bench level
- Instrument-specific protocols

### 7. Analytical Results
- Must satisfy all requirements traced through this chain
- Pre-audit verifies traceability at every level

## Traceability Requirement
For any given analytical result, the pre-audit system must be able to trace
the applicable requirement from the result back through each layer to the
authorizing CFR citation. Gaps in this chain = audit findings.
